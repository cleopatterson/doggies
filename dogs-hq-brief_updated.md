# Dogs HQ — Product Brief

## What is this?

A companion app for the **Parkyard** WhatsApp group — three Bulldogs NRL fans (Tony, Benny, Jordy) who chat every week about the Dogs. The app makes those conversations better by giving us context, debates, and a way to play armchair coach with accountability.

It's not a tipping comp. It's not a gambling app. It's not a news digest. It's a **conversation engine** for mates who are genuine fans.

## The group

- **Tony** (me) — runs this from my WhatsApp number via OpenClaw
- **Benny Barnett** — the deep knowledge guy, defends unpopular players, shares fan page memes
- **Jordy Barnett** — emotional reactor, first to text after a big win or loss, spots the details

The WhatsApp group is called **Parkyard**. Messages come from my number.

## Core concept

Every week, Claude analyses the upcoming Bulldogs game and generates content that fuels better conversations. Not generic NRL coverage — specifically tuned to what WE talk about: player debates, coaching decisions, emotional reactions, fan page takes.

## Features

### 1. Intel (pre-game context)

Accordion layout with three sections:

**Match Intel** — ins/outs/team news for both teams. Tagged badges: IN (green), OUT (red), WATCH (amber), FORM (blue), STAT (grey). Both Bulldogs and opponent.

**Player to Watch** — one player each week with a spotlight. Their stats, bio, and why they matter this week. Could be a debut (Leo Thompson), a returning captain (Crichton), or someone under pressure (Montoya). Player avatar with initials for now — **production version should scrape NRL headshot images from nrl.com's internal API** (the JS-rendered player pages load image URLs via XHR that can be intercepted).

**Head to Head** — 2-3 key individual matchups with stats on each side. Dogs player (blue card) vs opponent player (grey/red card). Brief analysis underneath.

### 2. Debates (the core interaction)

3-4 provocative questions per week generated by Claude based on analysis of the specific matchup, recent form, team selection, and fan sentiment. These should sound like something one of us would say in the group chat, not a sports journalist.

Examples of good debate prompts:
- "Montoya keeps his spot. Are we ok with this?" 🐢
- "Crichton back — but we've won 2 without him. Walk straight in?" 💪
- "Hot take: Connor Tracey is actually a good fullback" 1️⃣
- "Suncorp on a Saturday arvo — how worried are we?" ✈️

Each debate has:
- An emoji icon
- The question (bold, conversational tone)
- Context paragraph (2-3 lines with stats/reasoning)
- 3 sides to pick (each with an emoji and short label)
- After picking, you see where the other two boys landed

This is NOT betting. No points, no odds, no scoring. It's just: commit to a take on record so we can argue about it.

**Trivia Corner** — one Bulldogs history question per week at the bottom. Multiple choice, shows the answer and a fun fact immediately. Draw from club history going back to the Berries era.

### 3. Washup (post-game)

Drops Monday morning. Reads like how we actually talk in the group, not a match report.

- **Score banner** — result with WIN/LOSS badge
- **Headline** — one-liner capturing the vibe ("Did that really happen?" / "Hard to watch that first half")
- **Vibe paragraph** — 3-4 sentences written in our voice, referencing what the boys said in the chat
- **Talking points** — 5-6 bullet items with emoji icons. The things we're actually discussing: player performances, injuries, coaching decisions, controversial moments. Written with personality ("Montoya... still Montoya. Fan pages having a field day.")
- **From the fan pages** — a curated summary of the best/worst fan reactions from Facebook/Reddit. The brutal comedy gold that Benny shares screenshots of.

### 4. Coach Mode (new — not yet prototyped)

Each week after teams are named, each of us picks our own 1-17 from the Bulldogs extended squad.

**How it works:**
- Show Ciraldo's actual named team as the starting point
- Each user can make changes: drop players, promote from reserves, swap positions
- Changes are tracked as diffs from Ciraldo's team ("Tony dropped Montoya, moved Addo-Carr to wing")
- Before the game: side-by-side comparison of all three coaches' teams plus Ciraldo's actual team. Differences highlighted.
- After the game: Claude analyses match stats and gives each coach a verdict. "Tony wanted to drop Montoya. Montoya had 2 errors and -15 run metres. Tony's team: validated." / "Benny wanted Crichton off the bench. Crichton came on and scored. Benny knew."

**Data needed:** Full extended squad (21-23 players), position eligibility, Ciraldo's named 1-17 each week.

## Architecture

### Frontend
- **React** single-page app, mobile-optimised (max-width 480px — it's opened from WhatsApp on phones)
- **Styling:** Inline styles, Barlow Condensed for headings, Inter for body. Dark theme (bg: #060a10, cards: #0d1117). Bulldogs blue (#005eb8) as accent.
- **No external CSS frameworks.** Tailwind utility classes are available but inline styles have been working well.
- **Persistence:** `window.storage` API for the prototype (key-value, persists across sessions). Production version should use a simple backend store.
- Host on **Vercel** or **Railway** (Tony has Railway set up already)

### Data pipeline
- **Tuesday:** Scrape team lists from nrl.com when teams are named. The NRL site is JS-rendered so use Puppeteer/Playwright to intercept the internal API calls that load player data (including headshot image CDN URLs).
- **Tuesday:** Claude (Opus) analyses the matchup and generates: intel items, player spotlight, head-to-head matchups, debate prompts, trivia question. Output as structured JSON.
- **Post-game:** Scrape match result and player stats. Claude generates the washup content and coach mode verdicts.
- **Data sources:** nrl.com (team lists, player profiles, match stats), Zero Tackle (additional stats), The Kennel forum (fan sentiment — see below).

### The Kennel fan sentiment scraper
The Kennel (thekennel.net.au) is the biggest Bulldogs fan forum — 46.7K threads, 2.6M messages in the Bulldogs Discussion section alone. It's a XenForo forum that renders server-side (no Puppeteer needed — simple HTTP requests work).

#### Thread classification system

XenForo uses **thread prefix tags** that appear as styled labels on each thread. The Kennel uses these consistently:

| Prefix | What it means | Example | How we use it |
|--------|--------------|---------|---------------|
| `GAMEDAY` | Official match thread — one per game | "GAMEDAY Round 5 - Bulldogs vs Souths - Friday 3rd April - 4:05pm Accor Stadium" | Primary source for live reactions and post-match meltdowns |
| `Official` | Long-running player/topic threads | "Official Lachlan Galvin Discussion" | Track which players the fan base is debating |
| `News` | Breaking team news, injury updates | "News Does Tracey stay?" | Intel tab content |
| `Opinion` | Reactive hot takes, usually post-match | "Opinion Players that need the drop now", "Opinion Wheres our threat?" | Debate topics and washup sentiment |
| `Social Media` | External content being discussed | "Social Media Jahream Bula's contract won't be activated" | News and rumour tracking |
| (no prefix) | General discussion | "Why is everyone melting down after losing 2 games?" | General sentiment |

#### GAMEDAY thread pattern

The GAMEDAY threads follow a very consistent, machine-parseable naming convention:

```
GAMEDAY Round {X} - Bulldogs vs {Opponent} - {Day} {Date} - {Time} {Venue}
```

Example: `"GAMEDAY Round 5 - Bulldogs vs Souths - Friday 3rd April - 4:05pm Accor Stadium"`

Regex to match: `GAMEDAY\s+Round\s+(\d+)\s*-\s*Bulldogs\s+vs\s+(.+?)\s*-\s*(.+)`

One thread per game. The kickoff time is in the title, which is critical for splitting pre-game vs post-game content (see below).

#### Separating pre-game vs post-game content

The GAMEDAY thread serves both purposes — fans post before kickoff with predictions and nerves, then during and after the game with live reactions and post-mortems. The scraper splits based on the kickoff time embedded in the thread title:

**Pre-game posts** = any post in the GAMEDAY thread before kickoff time. These contain predictions, lineup reactions, nervous energy, and pre-game banter.

**Post-game posts** = any post after kickoff time. These contain live commentary, immediate reactions, and the post-match meltdown/celebration.

For the other thread types, timing works naturally:
- `Opinion` threads created **before** gameday → pre-game sentiment (lineup debates, coaching questions)
- `Opinion` threads created **after** the game → post-match reactive takes ("Players that need the drop now" always appears within hours of a loss)
- `Official` player threads spike in activity after games — check the last 2-3 pages for post-match takes on specific players

#### What to scrape and when

**Tuesday pre-game pipeline (after teams are named):**

1. Fetch Bulldogs Discussion forum index (pages 1-2): `?forums/bulldogs-discussion.4/` and `?forums/bulldogs-discussion.4/page-2` — gives the 40 most recently active threads with titles, reply counts, and timestamps
2. Fetch Rumours section index: `?forums/rumours.10/` — player signing chatter
3. Filter for threads active in the last 7 days
4. Classify by prefix tag (GAMEDAY / Official / Opinion / News / Social Media / general)
5. For the top 10 hottest threads by recent reply count, fetch the last 2-3 pages of replies
6. For the current week's GAMEDAY thread, fetch posts before kickoff time (pre-game only)
7. Bundle thread titles + reply excerpts as structured text → feed to Claude pre-game prompt

**Monday washup pipeline (morning after the game):**

1. Fetch Bulldogs Discussion forum index (pages 1-2) — find new threads created since the game
2. Find the GAMEDAY thread for the round just played
3. Fetch the last 5-6 pages of the GAMEDAY thread — this is where the live reactions and immediate post-match discussion lives
4. Find any new `Opinion` threads created in the 48 hours since the game — these are the reactive hot takes
5. Check the hottest `Official` player threads for recent post-game activity (e.g. the Galvin thread spiking after he scores a solo try but also misses a tackle)
6. Bundle all of it → feed to Claude washup prompt

#### Technical notes
- XenForo forum pages are paginated: `?forums/bulldogs-discussion.4/page-2`
- Thread pages: `?threads/thread-slug.ID/page-2`
- Thread prefixes render as `<span class="label">` elements in the HTML — easy to extract
- Posts are in `<article>` tags with `data-author` and timestamp attributes
- Each post has a `data-content` attribute with the post ID for deduplication
- No authentication required for reading the Bulldogs Discussion section
- Rate limit respectfully — 1 request per 2-3 seconds, cache aggressively
- Consider adding attribution in the app ("🏟️ Vibe check from The Kennel") to give credit
- The Kennel also has a podcast (available on Spotify/Apple) — could be a future data source via transcript

### WhatsApp integration
- **OpenClaw** bot running on Railway, connected to Tony's WhatsApp number via the Baileys library (already set up and tested — Tony has had it messaging him previously)
- The bot posts to the Parkyard group on a cron schedule:
  - **Thursday 6pm:** "🐶 DOGS HQ — Rd 8 v Broncos is live. Set your debates and pick your team → [link]"

## Tone guide

This app talks like a fourth member of Parkyard. Not a sports journalist. Not a gambling site. Not an AI.

**Do:** "Montoya keeps his spot. Are we ok with this?" / "Galvin ran riot. Origin halfback?" / "The fan pages are brutal."

**Don't:** "Analysis suggests Canterbury have a 67% win probability" / "Place your predictions for Round 8" / "Based on historical data..."

Debates should feel like something Benny would drop in the chat at 7:35am on Monday morning. The washup should read like you're catching up with a mate who watched the game.
